About the Project

Underwater images captured in natural aquatic environments frequently suffer from diverse quality degradations caused by wavelength-dependent light absorption and scattering. Common issues include blue/green tint, low illumination, hazy appearance, blurry details, noisy artifacts, high contrast, and raw (minimally processed) images.

This project develops a deep learning-based classification system that automatically detects and labels the dominant quality degradation present in an underwater image. Three convolutional neural network architectures — a custom CNN, MobileNet, and ResNet — are trained and compared using the benchmark UIEB dataset (890 raw underwater images with corresponding degradation characteristics).

The system classifies input images into one of nine classes: blue_tint, green_tint, red_tint, blurry, hazy, noisy, low_illumination, high_contrast, raw.
